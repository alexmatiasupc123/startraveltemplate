Miraflores

Description

The district of Miraflores is one of the forty-three districts that make up Lima, located in the department of the same name, in Peru. It is bordered to the north by the district of San Isidro, to the east by the districts of Surquillo and Santiago de Surco, to the south by the district of Barranco and to the west by the Pacific Ocean through the Costa Verde.

Miraflores ranks sixth in the Human Development Index of Peru's districts in 2019. It has many tourist attractions. Because of its degree of security, a high number of tourists from all over the world prefer it and for this same reason more and more hotels, including the most fashionable in the city of Lima, are located in this district.

In addition to its beaches and boardwalks with well-kept parks and a panoramic view of the entire bay of Chorrillos, it has several incredible sites such as the Huaca Pucllana, the central park of Miraflores, the Larcomar shopping center, the Ricardo Palma house-museum, among many others.

Relevant Data

  • Miraflores remains among the top three districts in Lima where it is most expensive to buy a house, land or apartment. It is inhabited mainly by families of high socioeconomic level.

  • It has a population of approximately 99,337 inhabitants.

  • Miraflores has an area of 9.62 square kilometers.
