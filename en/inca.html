Inca

Description

The Inca Empire was the largest empire in pre-Columbian America. The Inca emerged in the highlands of Peru in the early 13th century and the last days of resistance to the Spanish conquest occurred in 1572. The center of Inca culture was the city of Cusco, from where the Incas had ruled over 40,000 kilometers of roads and conquered hundreds of villages.

The Inca or sovereign was a military, political and religious leader; he had a semi-divine character, since he was venerated as the personification of the Sun God. His power was based on a kind of theocratic absolutism, which crowned a collectivist organization of society: in exchange for the obligation to support his subjects, the Inca imposed on them the duty to work in the construction of irrigation works, public buildings, bridges, tunnels and an extensive network of roads.

In the Quechua language, the territory of the Incas was called Tahuantinsuyu ("four parts"), so that their empire was the "Empire of the four parts of the world".

Relevant Data

  • According to the Inka origin myth, Inti, the sun, sent two of his children-Manco Capac and Mama Ocllo-in order to bring order and civilization to mankind. The pair emerged from Lake Titicaca and headed north to found a city. The city was to be Cusco and the trail they traveled, the first Inka Trail.

  • The architecture in all the constructions of the Inca empire sought mainly to show power.

  • The Incas were able to domesticate and cultivate about 3,000 varieties grouped into eight potato species, of which about 1,000 are now extinct.

  • Corn was adored by the Incas. This cereal was their main source of energy and wealth.
