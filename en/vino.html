Wine

Description

It is an alcoholic beverage obtained from grapes through alcoholic fermentation of its must or juice; fermentation is produced by the action of yeasts that transform the sugars of the fruit into ethyl alcohol and carbon dioxide.

In the 16th century, grapes arrived in Peru from the Canary Islands, brought by the Marquis Francisco de Caravantes. Chroniclers of the time point out that it was in the Marcahuasi hacienda, in Cuzco, where the first winemaking in South America took place.

Today, around 30 grape varieties are grown in Peru, including some of the historic European varieties such as cabernet sauvignon, pinot noir, petit verdot and chardonnay.

Relevant Data

  • The types of wines are segmented into four:

    1. Dry wine (red wine): The seco (red wine) of European origin, as well as in Chile and Argentina in South America serves to accompany meats and grills.

    2. Sweet wine: Sweet wines such as burgundy are used to accompany Creole food.

    3. White wine: White wines accompany all types of seafood, fresh and cold food.

    4. Sparkling wine (Champagne): The sparkling wine called the celebrity wine is used to accompany snacks.
