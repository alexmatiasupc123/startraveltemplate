Arequipa Cathedral

Description

The Cathedral of Arequipa is considered one of the first religious monuments of the XVII century in Arequipa. It was built with ignimbrite, and brick vaults; it is the main sanctuary of the city occupying the entire north side of the Plaza de Armas.

On August 15, 1540 the construction of the cathedral of Arequipa began by its founder Garci Manuel de Carbajal and was completed in 1656. It had 3 sections, 180 feet long by 84 feet wide (84.86 m by 25.6 m), 8 pillars, 5 chapels, 22 arches, 15 brick vaults and 15 vaults.

On December 1, 1844, there was a great fire in the Cathedral. The fire destroyed several parts of the church and many paintings, sculptures and furniture, as well as several vaults and pillars. Days later, on December 15, 1844, reconstruction work began under the direction of Bishop José Sebastián de Goyeneche y Barreda and his brother Juan Mariano de Goyeneche. The technical direction was entrusted to the architect Lucas Poblete. Part of it was rebuilt on the old church of San Juan.

Relevant Data

  • The Loret organ is the jewel of the Cathedral of Arequipa.

  • It is one of only 70 churches in the world authorized to display the Vatican banner.

  • Built on an area of at least two hectares, it occupies a surface of 7752 square meters; the approximate measurements of this temple are 61 meters wide by 128 meters long and a height of 67 meters to the top of the towers.
