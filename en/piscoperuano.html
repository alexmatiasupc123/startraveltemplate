Peruvian Pisco

Description

Pisco is an exclusively Peruvian brandy obtained by distilling fresh must from recently fermented "pisqueras "1 grapes, using traditional and ancestral quality methods, resulting in a pure production process, free of additives, sugars, water and other elements other than grapes.

It is produced only on the coast of the departments of Lima, Ica, Arequipa, Moquegua, and the valleys of Locumba, Sama and Caplina in the department of Tacna.

Pisco became a very popular drink among travelers in the region due to its very own characteristics, which determined that its benefits were widely spread to the point of being exported in the famous "botijas", in large quantities to neighboring regions.

Relevant Data

  • The Regulations of the Pisco Denomination of Origin recognize three types of Pisco:

    1. Pure Pisco: Pisco obtained exclusively from a single variety of "pisquera grape". Pisco

    2. Mosto Verde: Pisco obtained from the distillation of fresh musts of incompletely fermented "Pisco grapes".

    3. Pisco Acholado: Pisco obtained from the combination of "pisqueras grapes" or musts completely or incompletely fermented, or from the mixture of Piscos, the proportions of each mixture being chosen by the producer.
