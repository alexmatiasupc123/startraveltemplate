Humantay Lagoon

Description

Cusco offers its visitors a wide variety of attractions that simply leave them amazed through all the history that each one has. As you can imagine, the Humantay Lagoon is no exception, a beautiful turquoise lagoon simply cannot go unnoticed by nature lovers.

The Humantay Lagoon takes its name from the nearby snow-capped mountain (Humantay, 5,473 m) and is considered one of the most beautiful and sacred lagoons in Cusco. Its turquoise waters adorned with snow-capped Salkantay (6,270 m) whisper directly to you why it is so popular.

Relevant Data

  • We can see a considerable number of tourists visiting the Humantay Lagoon during the dry season, which runs from May to mid-October.

  • To get to the Humantay Lagoon from the city of Cusco, you must travel a road of about 120 km, passing through the towns of Limatambo and Mollepata, until you reach the community of Soraypampa. Once we enter Soraypampa, we begin a hike uphill for about 2 km until we reach the lagoon.

  • The entrance fee is paid in the town of Mollepata and it gives us access to the Soraypampa camp, where the lagoon is located.
